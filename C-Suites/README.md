## 4. Incident Response Plan

### 4.1 Incident Classification

| Severity | Definition | Response Time | Escalation |
|----------|------------|---------------|------------|
| **P0 - Critical** | Business down, data breach, legal emergency | < 15 min | CEO + All C-Suite |
| **P1 - High** | Major feature broken, security concern | < 1 hour | CTO + CIO + relevant |
| **P2 - Medium** | Partial outage, customer escalation | < 4 hours | Function owner |
| **P3 - Low** | Minor issues, non-urgent | < 24 hours | Function owner |

### 4.2 Incident Response Team

| Role | Primary | Backup | Contact |
|------|---------|--------|---------|
| Incident Commander | CEO | COO | [Contact] |
| Technical Lead | CTO | CIO | [Contact] |
| Communications Lead | CXA | CMO | [Contact] |
| Legal Advisor | CLO | - | [Contact] |

### 4.3 Response Procedures

#### Security Incident
1. CIO assesses scope and contains threat
2. CLO evaluates disclosure requirements
3. CXA prepares customer communication
4. CEO coordinates overall response

#### PR Crisis
1. CMO assesses situation
2. CLO reviews messaging
3. CXA handles direct communications
4. CEO provides final approval

#### Operational Failure
1. COO leads recovery efforts
2. CTO provides technical support
3. CXA communicates with affected customers
4. CFO assesses financial impact
